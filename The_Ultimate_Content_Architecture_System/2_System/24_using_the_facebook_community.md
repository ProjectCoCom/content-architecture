# Using the Facebook Community

### What This Is
The private Facebook community is an exclusive space for all customers of The Ultimate Content Architecture System. It's a place where you can connect with your peers, ask questions, share your successes, and get support on your journey to building a world-class content program. This is your "inner circle" of fellow content strategists and marketers who are all working towards the same goal.

This is more than just a support forum. It's a vibrant and active community of practice. It's a place for networking, for collaboration, and for collective learning. The goal is to create a space where you can feel supported, inspired, and motivated to do your best work.

By actively participating in this community, you can get more value out of this system, you can accelerate your learning, and you can build a valuable network of professional contacts.

You can see what's working for them, what's not, and what they are learning along the way. This can be a great way to get new ideas and to avoid common mistakes.

Furthermore, the Facebook community is a great place to build your professional network. You will have the opportunity to connect with a diverse group of content marketers from a wide variety of industries and backgrounds. These connections can be a valuable resource for your career for years to come.

### Community Guidelines
To ensure that our community is a safe, supportive, and valuable space for everyone, we have a few simple guidelines:

1.  **Be respectful.** We have a zero-tolerance policy for any kind of harassment, hate speech, or personal attacks.
2.  **No self-promotion.** This is a place for learning and for connection, not for selling your products or your services.
3.  **Stay on topic.** Please keep the conversation focused on content marketing and content strategy.
4.  **Give more than you take.** The best community members are the ones who are generous with their knowledge, their experience, and their support.

### Your Action Steps

**1. Join the Community and Set Your Notification Settings (5 minutes)**
Use your access link to join the community. Once you are in, take a moment to set your notification settings. We recommend setting them to "All Posts" for the first week to get a sense of the flow of the conversation.

> **Strategic Insight:** The first week in a new community can be overwhelming. Don't feel like you have to read every single post. Just try to get a feel for the people, the topics, and the overall vibe of the group.

**2. Introduce Yourself to the Community (10 minutes)**
Write a short post to introduce yourself. A good introduction post includes your name, your role, your industry, and one thing you are hoping to learn or to achieve with your content strategy. This is a great way to start building connections with the other members.

**3. Schedule Your Community Time (5 minutes)**
The best way to get value out of the community is to make it a habit. Block off 30 minutes on your calendar once or twice a week to check in on the community. This will help you to stay on top of the conversation and to be a more consistent participant.

> **Common Mistakes During This Step:**
> - **Being a "Lurker":** It can be tempting to just read the posts without ever participating. But the real value of the community comes from the interaction. Don't be afraid to jump in!
> - **Asking a Question That Has Already Been Asked:** Before you ask a question, use the search function to see if it has already been answered.
> - **Forgetting to Give Back:** If someone in the community helps you, make sure you thank them. And look for opportunities to help other people in the community.

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Violating the community guidelines.** Our guidelines are in place to keep the community safe and valuable for everyone. Please make sure you have read and understood them.
- **Being a "ghost."** If you join the community and then never participate, you are not going to get any value out of it. You have to be an active participant to get the benefits.
- **Asking for "brain-picking."** The community is a great place to get feedback and to ask for advice. But please be respectful of other people's time. Don't just ask for a free consultation.
- **Not having a clear profile.** Make sure your Facebook profile is professional and that it gives people a clear idea of who you are and what you do. This will make it much easier for you to build connections with the other members.
