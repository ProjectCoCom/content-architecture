### Your Action Steps

**1. Create Your "Master Strategy" Folder (5 minutes)**
In your company's shared drive, create a new folder called "Content Strategy - [Year]." This is where you will store all of your completed templates. This will be your single source of truth for your content strategy.

> **Strategic Insight:** A well-organized and accessible set of strategy documents is a hallmark of a professional content marketing program. It makes it much easier to onboard new team members, to get buy-in from stakeholders, and to keep your team aligned.

**2. Work Through the Foundational Templates (2-4 hours)**
Start by working your way through the templates in Part 1 of this guide (Content Mission, Audience Architecture, Competitive Analysis, and Core Topic Selection). These are the foundational elements of your content strategy, and you should complete them before you move on to the more tactical templates.

**3. Share Your Completed Templates with Your Team (30 minutes)**
Once you have completed the foundational templates, schedule a meeting with your team and with your key stakeholders to walk them through your work. This is a crucial step for getting feedback, for building alignment, and for getting everyone excited about the direction of your content strategy.

> **Common Mistakes During This Step:**
> - **Filling Out the Templates in a Silo:** These templates are most effective when they are filled out collaboratively. Get your team involved in the process.
> - **Treating the Templates as a "One and Done" Exercise:** Your content strategy is a living thing. You should be regularly reviewing and updating your templates as your business and your market evolve.
> - **Not Using the Outputs:** A completed template is not the goal. The goal is to use the outputs of the template to make smarter decisions about your content.

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Getting bogged down in the details of the templates.** The templates are a guide, not a straightjacket. The goal is not to fill out every single box perfectly; it's to have a productive and strategic conversation with your team.
- **Not adapting the templates to your specific needs.** These templates are designed to be a starting point. Feel free to add, remove, or change the questions to make them more relevant to your business.
- **Forgetting that the templates are just a means to an end.** The goal is not to have a folder full of beautifully filled-out templates. The goal is to have a successful content marketing program. The templates are just a tool to help you to get there.
- **Not having a clear owner for each template.** To ensure that the templates are being used effectively and are being kept up-to-date, it's a good idea to assign a clear "owner" for each one.
