# Chapter 7: Content Interlinking for SEO & User Experience

### What This Is

Content interlinking is the practice of strategically linking your pages to one another. It's the "connective tissue" of your content architecture, creating a cohesive web of information that is easy for both users and search engines to navigate.

### Why This Matters

Effective interlinking is critical for both SEO and user experience. From an SEO perspective, it helps search engines understand the structure of your site and the relationships between your different pieces of content. This can lead to better rankings and more organic traffic. From a user experience perspective, it helps guide readers to the information they're looking for, keeping them on your site longer and moving them through your conversion funnel.

Start with a simple "hub-and-spoke" model. Your pillar content is the "hub," and your cluster content and supporting articles are the "spokes." Every "spoke" should link back to the "hub," and the "hub" should link out to all of the "spokes." This creates a clear and logical structure that is easy for both users and search engines to follow.

At this stage, you'll start to think more about the context of your links. Instead of just having a "related articles" section at the bottom of your posts, you'll want to include contextual links within the body of your content. These are the most valuable types of links, as they provide both users and search engines with a clear signal about the topic of the linked page.

Advanced practitioners will create a comprehensive interlinking strategy that takes into account the user's journey, the authority of different pages, and the overall goals of the business. This could involve using tools to analyze your internal linking structure, identifying opportunities for improvement, and even creating a "link budget" to ensure that your most important pages are getting the most link equity.

### Interlinking Rules of Thumb

-   **Pillar to Clusters**: Your pillar page should link out to all of its corresponding cluster core articles.
-   **Clusters to Pillar**: Every cluster core article should link back up to its parent pillar page.
-   **Clusters to Supporting Content**: Cluster core articles should link to the supporting articles within that cluster.
-   **Supporting Content to Cluster**: Supporting articles should link back to their parent cluster core article.
-   **Cross-Cluster Linking**: Where relevant, link between related articles in different clusters.
