# 1. Defining Your Content Mission

### What This Is
Your content mission is the foundational statement that defines why your content exists, who it serves, and what transformation it provides. This isn't a tagline or marketing copyâ€”it's your strategic north star that guides every content decision you make.

### Why This Matters
Without a clear content mission, you'll create random articles that don't build authority or business value. Your mission ensures every piece of content you create ladders up to a larger strategic goal. It helps you say "no" to content ideas that don't serve your purpose and "yes" to ones that do.

### Your Action Steps
Your primary task is to draft a clear and concise content mission statement. This involves opening the "Content Mission Worksheet" and answering the three core questions: What problems does your content solve, who are you solving them for, and what outcome will the readers achieve? Based on these answers, you will write the first draft of your mission statement, which should be no more than two or three sentences. It's crucial to then share this draft with your team to gather feedback and ensure it aligns with everyone's understanding of the brand and its goals.

Once you have a draft, you need to validate it against your company's overarching business objectives. Review your key business goals for the quarter or year and ensure that your content mission directly supports at least two or three of them. If there's a misalignment, you'll need to adjust the statement. This step ensures that your content is not just creating noise but is actively working to help the business succeed.

Finally, you must test the mission's effectiveness as a decision-making tool. Write down a list of five potential content topics and, for each one, ask yourself if it supports the content mission. If you can't confidently say "yes" to at least four out of the five, your mission statement is likely too broad or vague and needs to be refined for better clarity and focus.
